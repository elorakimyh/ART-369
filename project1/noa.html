# of times i have visited: 4 times


dishes i have ordered: papaya salad, fried calamari, thai hot wing, noa drunken noodle, grilled flank steak pad see-ew, khao soi, pork belly


my recs: grilled flank steak pad see-ew and thai hot wing


comments: i definitely do think noa tastes better than it's parent restaurant september in bangkok. they do asian fusion so well while still keeping the flavors of traditional thai cuisine. i heard that they were known for their drunken noodles. while they were good, i thought the star of the show was their pad see-ew with flank steak. the steak really adds to the dish and it has the perfect tenderness and flavor. would highly recommend trying that when you go although the second time i had it it wasn't as good as the first.
